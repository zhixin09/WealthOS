# Singapore Household Liquidity

Singapore households with uneven cash inflows should prioritize a liquidity buffer that covers at least six to twelve months of fixed expenses before increasing exposure to illiquid assets. Short-duration cash instruments and insured deposits remain the preferred reserve layer when income volatility is rising.

Liquidity planning should not be separated from liabilities. Mortgage, education, and dependent-care obligations create non-discretionary cash burn that must be modeled before allocating excess capital to higher-volatility positions.

For affluent households, emergency reserves can sit alongside opportunity capital. The distinction matters because deploying all excess cash into risk assets reduces flexibility when rates, job conditions, or family obligations change quickly.
